Exploring global tea culture

Introduction

Tea is one of the world's most ubiquitous drinks, an essential component to cultural traditions and modern lifestyles far surpassing the unassumingly subtle flavor of the beverage itself. A bloody trail through history follows the evolution of tea as it disseminated from China; wars were fought and taxes levied as the popularity of the drink grew. Ideas flourished and movements formed around the teapot, the social gatherings inspired by the people whom tea had brought together. In each major tea culture, there is a distinctly different flavor to the interactions tea inspires, yet it somehow remains almost universally recognizable. This website is devoted to an in-depth study of the role tea has played in four countries: China, Russia, Japan, and Great Britain.
